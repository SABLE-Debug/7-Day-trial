— 02 / The look

Minimalism that moves.

Static composition is restrained. Motion is purposeful. Screenshots look minimalist. The live URL feels premium.

Layout

Restrained

Negative space. One serif headline, one mono label, one accent gradient. Nothing crowded.

Motion

One hero move

One animation per page that earns the wow. Plus micro-interactions on hover. That is it.

Effects

Spice, not meal

One gradient bloom in a corner. One subtle 3D tilt. No bouncy springs, no scroll hijacking.

— 03 / The work

AI does seventy percent.

Brief, curate, polish, ship. 30–60 minutes per deliverable. You do the judgement; the tools do the lift.

Step 1 · draft

Claude Design

First-draft layouts, components, and code in 5–15 minutes instead of 1–2 hours.

Step 2 · imagery

Krea · MJ

Hero imagery, 3D objects, motion loops. The ribbon and orbit on this page were both Krea.

Step 3 · copy

Voice-locked

Claude writes in the brand voice — banned-word list enforced, one italic accent per paragraph.

Step 4 · ship

Polish · deliver

You finish, you judge, you ship. 30 minutes of human taste on top of the AI draft.

— 04 / The moats

What defends.

Speed is table stakes — anyone copies it in 30 days. Taste, reputation, and distribution compound.

01 · distribution

Weekly teardown

Every Monday, a public before/after redesign of one real brand. 45 minutes. Highest-converting design marketing that exists.

02 · proof

Logo wall

Every signed client adds a logo to the landing page. After 6, conversion doubles.

03 · authority

Open tokens

Publish the design tokens publicly. Become the reference standard for editorial-AI design.

04 · referral

One month free

Client refers a signed client — both get a free month. ~40% of new clients arrive this way.

05 · lock-in

Grandfathered

First 20 clients lock $3,995 forever. Creates urgency, kills churn.

06 · trust

Testimonial wall

One-sentence client quotes, published with avatar + company. Closes hesitant prospects faster than a call.

— 06 / Two paths

Pick a pace.

Same total work. Path A spreads it across 7 days. Path B compresses it into 6. Path B burns hotter.

Path A — Aggressive
First sale · Day 7
Day 0
Foundation, ~4 hrs. Domain, Pages live, Stripe links, queue board, profiles updated, Cal link, email forwarder.
Day 1
Source 25 prospects, ~3 hrs. Funded brands from Crunchbase, recent raises, Product Hunt. Filter by aesthetic.
Day 2
Spec 10 mockups, ~5 hrs. Quick homepage redesigns in Claude Design, ~30 min each. MOBIUS aesthetic, instantly recognizable.
Day 3
Send 10 DMs, ~1 hr. Three-line message + mock attached. 7am SAST. Mix LinkedIn / X / email.
Day 4
Spec + send 10 more, ~5 hrs. Repeat for prospects 11–20. First replies land — answer within 1 hour.
Day 5
Calls + final batch, ~3 hrs. Book inbound into same-day slots. Send last 5 specs.
Day 6
Close window. First booked call usually lands here. Send Stripe link mid-call.
Day 7
First payment. Onboard via queue, ship first deliverable within 48 hrs, fill a portfolio case slot.
Path B — Hyper
First sale · Day 6
Day -1
All foundation done. Stripe, Pages, queue, profiles — 4 hrs before the clock starts.
Day 0
Source 30 + spec all of them in one 8-hour block. 15 min per mockup, AI-heavy.
Day 1
Send 30 DMs at 7am SAST, batched across LinkedIn / X / email.
Day 2
Reply storm. Every reply handled within 30 min. Push for same-day calls.
Day 3
Calls back-to-back in 30-min slots.
Day 4
Close attempts on every call. Stripe link sent live during the call.
Day 5
First payment received.
Day 6
First deliverable shipped, portfolio case slot filled.

Trade-off: Path B is ~50 hours of intense work in 6 days. Higher burnout risk, and 30 DMs in one batch hits spam flags faster than 10/day. Pick it only if you can sustain the sprint.
