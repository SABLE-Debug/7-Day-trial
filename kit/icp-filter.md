# ICP FILTER — AI SERIES A/B SAAS

The 7-day test runs against one buyer. Everyone else is noise.

---

## THE BUYER

AI-native Series A or B startup. Raised in the last 12 months. $5–30M ARR. 10–50 employees. Technical founders. No design team. Public on Twitter, Dribbble, and founder Slack groups.

That is the only profile that gets a Loom.

---

## SCORING RUBRIC

Each prospect gets a score out of 100. Five gates, twenty points each. *Score is bounded — a prospect cannot pass with a zero in any gate.*

### Gate 1 — Funding (20 pts)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| Series A or B announced in last 12 months | 20 |
| Series A or B announced 12–24 months ago | 12 |
| Seed with $5M+ raised, AI-native | 8 |
| Pre-seed, bootstrapped, or older than 24mo | 0 |

Source: Crunchbase, TechCrunch, the lead investor's portfolio page.

### Gate 2 — ARR + Headcount (20 pts)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| $5–30M ARR, 10–50 employees | 20 |
| $3–5M ARR, 5–15 employees | 12 |
| $30–60M ARR, 50–100 employees | 8 |
| Outside both bands | 0 |

Source: Glassdoor headcount, LinkedIn employee count, public revenue claims.

### Gate 3 — AI-native (20 pts)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| Core product is an AI agent, model wrapper, or AI-first workflow | 20 |
| AI is a major feature but not the core | 10 |
| AI is a marketing skin on a non-AI product | 4 |
| Not AI in any defensible way | 0 |

Source: their landing page hero copy, their G2 category, their last fundraise narrative.

### Gate 4 — Founder profile (20 pts)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| Technical founder, ships publicly, posts on X | 20 |
| Technical founder, low public profile | 12 |
| Non-technical founder, AI-fluent | 8 |
| Non-technical founder, AI-blind | 0 |

Source: founder's X, GitHub, Substack, podcast appearances.

### Gate 5 — Brand vacuum (20 pts)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| No design hire on LinkedIn, brand obviously templated | 20 |
| One design hire, work feels rushed | 14 |
| Small design team, work is decent but inconsistent | 6 |
| Full brand team, polished system already | 0 |

Source: LinkedIn employee filter on "design," then a 60-second audit of their site, app, deck, and last three social posts.

## DECISION

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| 85–100 | Loom this week. Top of the queue. |
| 70–84 | Loom within two weeks. Research dossier first. |
| 50–69 | Park. Revisit at Day 30 review. |
| Under 50 | Cut. Do not Loom. |

Three whales beat thirty minnows. Always.

---

## DISQUALIFIERS — INSTANT CUT

A prospect with any of these is removed from the list regardless of score.

- Public layoffs in the last 90 days
- Founder under investigation, in a public spat, or visibly burning out
- Already retains a brand studio (check Dribbble, check Working Not Working)
- Sells to consumer (B2C) — wrong buyer pattern
- Headquartered in a sanctioned country
- Bootstrapped with founder hostility to agencies (read their X)

---

## WORKFLOW

1. Pull a raw target list from Crunchbase, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, or a founder Slack.
2. Run each name through the rubric. Two minutes per name, no more.
3. Save the score and the one-sentence rationale to `prospects/scores.csv`.
4. Sort descending. Loom the top five. Park the rest.

The rubric is the only filter that ships. *Conviction is downstream of the score.*
